Dark Autumn

"The Grounded Earth Goddess"
Warm
Undertone
Dark
Value
Rich
Chroma
Dark Autumn sits at the intersection of True Autumn and Dark Winter — the deepest and most dramatic of the Autumn family. Your colouring carries a naturally rich, warm glow: think deep forest greens, terracotta, dark chocolate, and burnished gold. Colors work best when they echo that depth and warmth rather than fighting it.

Your Features

Eyes
Dark hazel, warm dark brown, dark green, or warm black. Look for swirling patterns and iris freckles. High contrast between iris and whites.
Hair
Medium to dark golden brown, dark auburn, dark chocolate, or black-brown. Develops warm golden or red highlights in the sun.
Skin
Neutral to neutral-warm undertones. Range from light to deep. Golden or bronze undertones visible. May have freckles; tans easily and warmly.

Pattern Guide

✓

Large autumn leaves, paisley, oval shapes, wood grain — organic and loosely arranged.

Dark background with warm-toned motifs. Reflects your natural feature contrast.

✕

Too structured — fights your organic warmth. Avoid rigid lines and cool-toned plaids.
✕

Get lost at your natural contrast level. Patterns need scale to register.

Outfit Formula

Value Contrast is Your Friend

Pair deep darks with warm mediums — like dark chocolate with camel, or forest green with mustard. You can also use hue contrast: yellows with blues-greens, or terracotta with teal. Avoid going head-to-toe in one mid-tone; your high-contrast features need an outfit that can hold their own.

Dark chocolate · Camel · Mustard

Earth + Accent

Ground the look in a neutral (chocolate, camel, ivory), then add one Autumn accent — rust, forest green, or teal. Three-colour maximum for the most polished result.

Plum · Warm cream · Deep gold

Dark Autumn vs. Dark Winter

You're Dark Autumn if…
Gold flatters more than silver. You look best in earthy, warm tones. Cool or icy colors feel stark rather than crisp on you. Your palette has an organic, burnished quality.
Dark Winter looks like…
Cool, blue-black pigments with icy clarity. Silver is the better metal. Pure black and pure white work. The palette is sharp and cool rather than rich and warm.

What to Avoid

Icy pastels

Too light and cool — creates a washed-out effect

Stark white

Swap for ivory, warm cream, or soft ecru

Bright pink / fuchsia

Cool-toned — clashes with your warm undertone

Dusty blue / periwinkle

Too cool and muted — loses your warmth entirely

Cool grey / silver

Replace with warm taupe, camel, or mushroom
